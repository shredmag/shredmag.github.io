NOTHING SEXIER THAN BEING SUSTAINABLE

Inside the world of wings vintage




















A 2022 survey found four in ten consumers purchased second-hand goods, while another one-third sold their used items on the second-hand market.


The trend of buying and selling second-hand clothing is well and truly in its prime, but it does not stop there. Most second-hand shoppers will tell you that sustainable shopping has an irreversible effect on the conscience and the bank account. In particular, young adults and students have realised that the ethical side of recycling clothing pairs nicely with the low-cost factor. Social media plays a significant role in spreading the word about sustainable fashion. Fil-a-bag and kilo sale events flooding Instagram and TikTok feeds motivate relaxed, ethical shoppers to be proactive.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        

THE TRIP


After doing my research, I was eager to feel the vibe for myself and get a taste of what it is like to be committed to second-hand living- I still have my weak moments as a high-store shopper. I needed to place myself in the center of the action at the 'Wings World' shop on Euston Street. So, I lured my housemate Paige out with the promise of bargain-basement-priced vintage numbers, and we set off on a journey of trains and tubes. Despite my lousy sense of direction and toxic relationship with Google Maps, we arrived around midday.


Luckily, it is hard to miss the large 'WINGS WORLD' sign above the entrance. After three pulls on a push door, we followed a staircase down to an actual basement. I use the term 'basement' lightly, as it was far from the typical dimly lit and haunted one you see in horror films. The space is limited but far from cramped; it gives the feel of a perfectly curated walk-in wardrobe. Immediately, Megan greeted us. I was instantly pleased to see the business owner be so involved with the physical work that takes place. It seems she thrives off of the community aspect of her work and likes to stay invested in it. Megan welcomed us, asked if we had been before and then said if we needed any help to let her know. She set the standard of a warm and friendly environment, and the shop's atmosphere reflected her throughout.




















A unique feature of Wings World was the pricing system. There are colour-coded price signs around the room linked to the colour of the tag on each item. It is simple to follow and reassuring to your bank account that you won't overspend from the get-go, given the prices range from £1.99 to £15. We followed the standard routine of second-hand shopping, doing laps around the rails, each time looking with more determination. After doing my daily 10,000 steps in the same four walls, Paige and I agreed that we had stripped the rails of our best finds. Approaching the till, the rest of the staff shared Megan's down-to-earth energy. Bonding over matching striped jumpers with a team member was a highlight and filled me with a semi-fashionable feeling that fed my ego. 


The journey home gave us time to reflect. Putting aside the honest prices and quality clothes, we agreed that the community aspect shapes Wing's vintage. The Wings Vintage regulars will have experienced the same satisfying conversation and sustainable fashion blend we had left with that day.
