FASHION THAT LASTS

 Autumn fashion for all year round


As we transition from summer to autumn, it’s time for everyone in cooler climates to start digging out their thicker clothes. Whether you’re a hoodie-only warrior or style yourself like the Michelin man, here’s some tips on what to look out for if you’re seeking out long lasting pieces for this season:


TRENCH COATS















Trench coats were historically military coats: heavy duty and well-suited for wet conditions. Now a staple in many wardrobes, the trench coat has been reimagined with every fashion era since its conception. Regardless of whether you prefer your trench coat short, billowing, or even made of a totally different material, it’s important to make sure your trench coat fits right and its stitching is well done.


When shopping for a trench coat, keep in mind how you plan to style it. If you envision yourself wearing it over thick layers, be sure to wear something like a large hoodie or jumper when you’re trying it on. Not only is it important to ensure you like how the trench coat looks over your jumper, but it is also important to ensure that you can actually fit the thick layers into the sleeves.


As with any garment, look out for the neatness of stitching. Are the stitches straight and not crossing over each other messily? Are there any skipped stitches, meaning any unusual gaps in the stitch pattern or loose thread in the stitch? Make sure the fabric lays flat when you wear the coat and that the stitches aren’t puckering. While these details are small, they all point to how much care was put into making the garment. You won’t want to pay high end prices for something poorly constructed, regardless of label.


KNIT JUMPERS















The knit jumper can be one of the most versatile pieces in your wardrobe. The most important part of picking a jumper is comparing the material list with the price. With knit jumpers, the ideal is 100% wool since they’re more breathable and won’t pill as readily. If you fall in love with an acrylic piece, make sure that you’re prepared to do the upkeep and that the price feels worthwhile to you. 


Beyond price, you’ll want to feel the texture of the jumper against your skin and consider the thickness. If you want your jumper to be your thickest layer, make sure the jumper is actually thick – beware of deceptive jumpers that are thinner than they look. Check how transparent they look under direct light by putting your hand under the fabric if you can’t try it on the changing rooms.


BELTS








When purchasing a belt, formal or casual, make sure you wear a pair of well-fitting pants with belt loops to ensure that the belt will fit around your waist or hips. Use the sizing tags on belts for a good starting point, and make sure the length is appropriate for what you want. With formal belts, you want to make sure the belt doesn’t loop around your waist so much that it feels bulky. 


Consider researching styles of buckle as they can vary greatly. If you want a safe option, the pin buckle is a classic style of belt buckle for both formal and casual belts. Anchor buckles tend to be more on the bold side. All belt buckles come in different finishes, so jewellery wearers may want to consider matching their favourite metal of their jewellery to their belt buckle.


You will also want to test the belt by sitting and walking in it, like trying on shoes. Is the belt comfortable when moving around? Consider whether or not you prefer fashion, function, or a balance of the two, especially with chunky buckles. You’re going to be wearing it so make sure you’ll be comfortable in it. 



Don’t forget: these are guidelines, not rules. The most important part of shopping for clothes is finding beautiful things that will last you a long time and are worth your money.
