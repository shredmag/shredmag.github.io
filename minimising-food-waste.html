MINIMISING FOOD WASTE

How on earth do you minimise your food waste when you’ve got no garden? Has all this talk about compost and fertiliser got you in a crap mood?



SHRED’s Top Tips for Minimising Food Waste:


1. STOCK IS YOUR BEST FRIEND

Grab all your vegetable scraps and shove them in a container in the fridge. I’m talking onion peel, carrot ends, anything you would normally throw. When you have enough, pop the scraps, salt and spices of your choice and enough water in the biggest pot you can - simmer until you’ve achieved a flavour you enjoy (which should take up to two hours). Refrigerate or freeze to have at a later date.



2. PLANNING AND MEAL PREPPING

The more you plan ahead, the less wastage you’ll have – meal prep your favourite lunch to have a couple times a week, freeze a few portions, and you’ve saved yourself money too.



3. EVERYTHING SOUP

This is where the veggie stock comes in super handy. A personal favourite meal of mine – broth, chowder, consommés, purée, bisque… the choices are endless. The flavours are in your fridge. 



4. EVERYTHING PASTA

Again, using what you have at the end of the week is a great meal idea, especially if, like me, you end up changing your mind on a meal prep and are left with some random bits and bobs. Invest in the biggest sack of penne available and the world is your oyster. 



5. EVERYTHING FRIED RICE

Add your favourite sauce, whatever you have left, and because you’ll definitely misjudge the amount of rice to make, eat the leftovers for lunch the next day.



6. NO MYSTERY ITEMS

Knowing what you have at all times is key to minimising waste. Pretend you are a private chef and catalogue your stash!



7. ONLY BUY WHAT YOU NEED (AND A FEW TREATS)

This goes hand in hand with meal planning and prepping. Of course, make sure you always have the basics in (canned things, emergency baked beans) and grab a few sweet treats.



8. CHECK YOUR PERISHABLES DAILY

If you notice something may be going bad, use it as soon as you can. Freeze your meat, and put vegetables in stock.

Squishy fruit makes great syrup – boil it with sugar and add to a sterilised jar. Enjoy with lemonade, water, ginger ale and more. See our recipe for Cheong here. 


9.(MOST) EXPIRY DATES ARE FOR THE WEAK

Use your nose, your eyes, and freeze things you know you won’t use asap. However, if in serious doubt - throw it out!


-


The best way to minimise food waste is to share what you have. Invite friends over for a leftovers party, or donate tins you’ll never use to a food bank. Give Food is a great charity that can help you find your local food bank- helping out is great but there is absolutely no shame in requesting anything you might need too.
