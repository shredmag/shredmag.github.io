CHEONG AND HOW TO MAKE IT

What is Cheong?










Cheong (청) is a syrup made from combining fruit with sugar and letting it sit in a jar to extract as much flavour as possible into the syrup. You can leave it for as little as a week, or up to three months, depending on how much you make.


HOW DO I MAKE IT?

First, thoroughly wash your chosen fruit with baking soda to completely clean it. Whilst waiting for the fruit to dry, weigh out your sugar – it should be equal to the weight of the fruit.


Classic method:

Add both to the jar in alternating layers, with a hefty amount of sugar on the very top to keep it free of mould. As the fruit breaks down, the liquid combines with the sugar for a sweet, syrupy taste. However, keep in mind that it will take a couple of weeks to start turning into syrup if you make a couple kilos worth.


Quick method:

The easiest and fastest way to achieve syrupy status is to muddle and squish the fruit – not all of it, but enough that it releases plenty of liquid. Add a good amount of the sugar and mix until it dissolves.


Again, keep back about a third of the sugar and pour it on top, sealing the cheong in the jar.










Make sure you store the jar in a cool, dark place so the flavour can intensify.


You can either strain it to make a syrup, or keep it as is if the fruit bits don’t bother you. If you strain it, keep back the pulp and make jam!


IDEAS FOR USING CHEONG:

-Drip into salad dressings

-Pour onto pancakes

-Drizzle on desserts

-Stir into a drink 


Cheong can be kept in the fridge for about 6 months. Get creative with flavours, or just use whatever you have! 


IDEAS FOR FLAVOURS OF CHEONG:

-Plum

-Lemon

-Peach

-Blueberry (especially good with pancakes)

-Apple and lemon

-Ginger and lemon (great for a hot drink)

-Peach and mango

-Strawberry (add to a matcha latte)
